How we view the project currently:

Norwegian income inequality fell dramatically from the onset of the second world war, possibly coming from two main sources:

  1. direct effect of war related investments
  2. a general surge in prices of food and raw material benefitting low skilled workers.

Comment by Eirik: Somehow, direct 'destruction', produced by war and a complete stop in world trade, clearly shocked the incomes and wealth of the very rich down. This is the conventional story that Piketty use to explain the drop in top income shares. We also see this in our data.

We trace the decline in inequality to its sources, by by did it not bounce back up immediately?

  • Increase in levels and progresivity of income and wealth taxation.
    • Mentioned by Ran: Taxing the Rich: A History of Fiscal Fairness in the United States and Europe. Kenneth Scheve and David Stasavage link. See also figure 1 here.
    • Nationalization of industry.
    • Trade unions. See figure 2 here.
    • 'The regulation economy'.
    • Welfare state? Schooling?
